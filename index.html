Here's a time line of Dr. Kalam's life

  • 1931 - Born in Rameswaram, India.
  • 1958 - Kalam earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology and joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • 1958-1980 - He soon moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation, where he was project director of the SLV-III, India’s first indigenously designed and produced satellite launch vehicle.
  • 1982 - Rejoining DRDO, Kalam planned the program that produced a number of successful missiles, which helped earned him the nickname “Missile Man.”
  • 1992-1997 - Kalam was scientific adviser to the defense minister.
  • 1998 - His prominent role in the country’s nuclear weapons tests established Kalam as a national hero, although the tests caused great concern in the international community.
  • 1999-2001 - Served as principal scientific adviser to the government with the rank of cabinet minister.
  • 2002 - Kalam was become India’s 11th president.
  • 2002-2007 - He remained committed to using science and technology to transform India into a developed country.
